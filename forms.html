✅ Before you submit a claim

Most reward issues resolve themselves within 48–72 hours. Before submitting a claim, check these things first:

  • Wait 48–72 hours. Tracking for many offers — especially app installs and subscriptions — takes up to 3 days to register after you complete the action. If you just completed the offer, wait before submitting.
  • Check you clicked from our platform. If you navigated to the offer directly (via bookmark, browser history, or by typing the URL), your completion may not have been tracked. The click must originate from the offerwall or platform link.
  • Check your spam folder. Reward notifications and confirmation emails sometimes land in spam.
  • Check your account balance. Your reward may already be there — log in and check before submitting a claim.
  • Disable VPN or proxy. Using a VPN or proxy during offer completion is a common reason for tracking failures. Make sure these are turned off when you complete an offer.
  • Ad blockers. Some ad blockers prevent tracking pixels from firing. Try completing offers in a clean browser or private window without extensions.
If you have checked all of the above and still haven't received your reward after 72 hours, go ahead and submit a claim →

⚙️ How claims are processed

When you submit a claim, it goes through the following stages:

1
Automatic pre-checks
We check for duplicate claims, fraud indicators, and whether the conversion was already tracked. If it was, you'll be approved without a full review.
2
Fast-track or manual review
Simple low-value claims (under $5.00) with valid proof are often approved automatically. Higher-value claims or complex cases are reviewed by our team.
3
Advertiser contact (if needed)
If we need to confirm your completion with the advertiser, we contact them directly. This takes up to 28 days. We follow up automatically on Day 14 and Day 21.
4
Outcome and credit
You receive an email with the outcome. If approved, your credit appears in your account within 24 hours.
Most claims are resolved within 3 business days. Advertiser disputes can take up to 28 days.

🎁 Reward not received

Most common causes

  • Tracking delay. Most offers credit within 24 hours, but some advertisers take up to 7 days. Wait at least 72 hours before submitting.
  • You completed the offer but the tracking didn't fire. This is the most common scenario. Submit a claim with your proof of completion and we'll investigate.
  • Reward was already issued to a different account. If you have multiple accounts, the reward may have gone to the wrong one. Contact support with both account IDs.
  • The reward was reversed. Some advertisers reverse credits after initial approval if they later detect an issue. You'll receive a notification if this happens — submit a claim to dispute it.
  • You didn't complete all required steps. Some offers require specific actions — like keeping the app installed for 30 days, or making a minimum purchase. Check the offer terms.
Tip: The most important thing you can do is keep proof. Take a screenshot immediately after completing an offer — app installed and running, subscription confirmation email, purchase receipt. This is critical if you need to claim later.

📡 Tracking issues

Why tracking fails

  • VPN or proxy was active. Always disable VPN and proxy tools before clicking an offer and keep them off until the required action is complete.
  • Ad blocker blocked the tracking pixel. Tracking works through a small pixel that fires when you complete the offer. Ad blockers can prevent this. Try completing offers in a clean browser.
  • You didn't click from our platform. The tracking link is unique to our platform. Opening the offer directly bypasses tracking entirely.
  • Multiple clicks or devices. If you clicked the offer link more than once or switched devices, tracking can get confused. Only use one device and click the offer link once.
  • Delayed conversion. Some offers — especially app installs and trial subscriptions — have a conversion delay. The advertiser only confirms the action after a set period (e.g. 7 days of app usage). This is normal.
If you don't have a click ID, don't worry — include it if you have it, but we can often recover it. Submit your claim with as many details as possible.

⚙️ Technical problems

Offer didn't load or redirect failed

  • Try a different browser. Some offers are not compatible with all browsers. Chrome or Firefox usually work best.
  • Clear cache and cookies for the offer site and try again.
  • Disable browser extensions — especially ad blockers, privacy tools, or script blockers.
  • Check your network. Some offers restrict access by region or require a stable connection.

App crashed during the offer

If an app crashed after you completed the required action but before it was tracked, submit a claim with a screen recording or screenshot of the error. Include the exact time of the crash if possible.

A screen recording of the issue is the most effective proof for technical claims. If you experienced a redirect failure, take a screenshot of the page you landed on.

📋 Advertiser disputes

Sometimes an advertiser rejects a claim even though you completed the offer correctly. This happens for several reasons:

  • The advertiser says you didn't qualify. This usually means a mismatch between what you completed and the advertiser's qualification criteria — e.g. they required a new customer and you already had an account.
  • The advertiser's terms changed. Advertiser terms sometimes change after an offer is listed. If this affected your completion, submit a dispute with the original offer terms if you have them.
  • Attribution window expired. Advertisers have a set window in which they accept conversions. If you completed the action after this window, they may reject it.

What we do when you dispute

We contact the advertiser directly and present your evidence. We follow up automatically at Day 14 and Day 21. If the advertiser does not respond within 28 days, we apply our default resolution policy.

The stronger your proof, the better your outcome. Include: the confirmation email, a receipt, the subscription screen — anything that shows you completed the required action.

❌ My claim was rejected

Your rejection notification will include the specific reason. Here are the most common reasons and what they mean:

  • "Offer not completed according to advertiser terms." The advertiser reviewed your completion and determined it didn't meet their requirements. Check the offer's terms and conditions.
  • "Conversion already tracked — reward was issued." Our system found the conversion. Check your account balance — your reward may already be there.
  • "Proof does not match the offer requirements." The screenshot or document you submitted doesn't clearly show the completed action. You may be given a chance to resubmit with better proof.
  • "Duplicate claim." You already have an open claim for this offer. You cannot submit two claims for the same offer at the same time.
  • "Submitted too early." You submitted before the 48-hour tracking window had passed. You will be given a window to resubmit after the tracking delay.
Always read the full rejection message — it will tell you whether you are eligible to resubmit and how to do so.

🔄 Can I resubmit a rejected claim?

Resubmission is not automatic. It is only available in specific situations, and your rejection notification will tell you if you are eligible.

Submitted before 48-hour tracking delayYes — resubmit after the window passes
Technical error caused incomplete submissionYes — you will be invited to resubmit cleanly
Advertiser rejected your completionNo — but you can request a second escalation by contacting support
Offer terms not metNo — the offer requirements were not satisfied

↩️ Withdrawing a claim

You can withdraw a claim while it is in Received or Action needed status. Once a claim is under review or with an advertiser, it cannot be withdrawn.

To withdraw, go to your claims history and select "Withdraw this claim". A withdrawn claim does not count as a duplicate — you can submit a new claim for the same offer afterwards.

If you accidentally submitted a duplicate, the system will block it automatically. You don't need to withdraw anything — just use your original claim.

💬 Frequently asked questions

How long does a claim take to resolve? ▼

Most claims resolve within 1–3 business days. Simple low-value claims with clear proof are often approved automatically within hours. Claims that require us to contact the advertiser can take up to 28 days — we follow up automatically at Day 14 and Day 21 if the advertiser doesn't respond.

I completed the offer but it shows as "not tracked" — what do I do? ▼

First, wait 48–72 hours — tracking for many offers takes time to register. If it still hasn't tracked after that, check that you clicked from our platform (not a direct link or bookmark), had no VPN or ad blocker active, and completed the offer on the same device you clicked from. If all of those are fine, submit a claim — include your click ID if you have it, plus a screenshot of your completed action.

Why was my claim rejected? ▼

The most common reasons are:

  • The offer was not completed according to the advertiser's terms (e.g. existing customer, wrong region, required steps not finished)
  • The reward was already issued — check your account balance
  • The proof you uploaded didn't match the offer
  • The claim was submitted before the 48-hour tracking window

Your rejection notification will always include the specific reason. Check it carefully — it will also tell you if resubmission is available.

Can I resubmit a rejected claim? ▼

It depends on the rejection reason. If you submitted wrong proof, selected the wrong offer, or submitted too early, you will be given a resubmission window. If the advertiser reviewed and rejected your completion, resubmission is not available — but you can contact support for a second escalation. Your rejection notification will tell you exactly what is available in your case.

The advertiser says I didn't qualify — can I still get my reward? ▼

Yes, in many cases. Submit a claim with your proof of completion and we will contact the advertiser on your behalf. We present your evidence directly and request a review. If the advertiser approves after escalation, your reward is issued. If they don't respond within 28 days, we apply our resolution policy. Stronger proof significantly improves your outcome — include any confirmation emails, receipts, or screenshots you have.

I submitted a claim by mistake — can I cancel it? ▼

Yes, while the claim is in "Received" or "Action needed" status. Go to your claims history and select "Withdraw this claim". Once a claim is under review or with an advertiser, it cannot be withdrawn. A withdrawn claim does not count as a duplicate, so you can resubmit for the same offer afterwards.

What proof do I need to upload? ▼

It depends on the offer type:

  • App install: screenshot of the app open on your device with your username visible
  • Subscription: the confirmation email or the subscription page showing your account and plan
  • Purchase: order confirmation or receipt with order number and your name or email
  • Technical issue: screenshot of the error or a screen recording

Accepted formats: JPG, PNG, PDF, GIF, MP4, MOV. Max 20 MB per file, up to 5 files. Proof is required for all claims above $5.00.

How do I check the status of my claim? ▼

You will receive an email update at every status change. You can also use the status checker on the Submit a claim page — enter your claim ID (from your confirmation email) to see the current status and what happens next.

I have multiple claims — can I submit them all at once? ▼

Each claim must be submitted separately for a specific offer. You can have multiple open claims for different offers at the same time. You cannot have two open claims for the same offer — the second will be blocked as a duplicate.

My reward was approved but I haven't received it — what do I do? ▼

Approved credits appear in your account within 24 hours. If it has been more than 24 hours, check your account balance first — it may be there but not prominently displayed. If it's still not there after 48 hours, contact support with your claim ID and we will investigate the credit issuance.

Why does the form look different depending on where I submit from? ▼

The claim form adapts based on the platform you use — Lootup app, PixelPoint web, Facebook Messenger, or the offerwall. Different platforms collect different information. For example, the app form asks for your device type; the Facebook form asks for your country; the offerwall pre-fills your user ID and offer name automatically. The form only shows fields that are relevant to your specific source, keeping it as simple as possible.
